The forgotten team

Qatar's preparation for the world cup have shined a spotlight on that army of workers who have done nothing less than redraw the country over the past decade, as well as on the system that exploits their desperation, and has cost thousands of them their lives.
The work force is so large and drown from so many places that its numbers seem impossible to count. it is a group so anonymous that, to this day no one is able to agree how many of its member died to get the world cup across the finish line. Read more

Alcohol Ban

Alcohol sales was banned from the stadiums which caused a lot of uproar. "Alcohol will not be served at the world cup's eight stadiums said the organizers two days before the tournament had started" The last minute decision made a lot of people upset and concerned with FSA (football supporters assocciation) saying "some fans like a beer at a game and some don't, but the real issue is the last minute u-turn which speaks to a wider problem- the total lack of communications and clarity from the organising commitee to the supporters" said an (FSA) spokesperson. what-happens-to-qatar-world-cup-stadiums?

According to a statement made by the supreme commitee's secretary General Hassan AL Thawadi. some of the stadiums will be dismantled and recycled. others will be dowmsized, and some transform into residential and shopping destinations. one specific stadium called (974) which is made up of 974 recycled shipping containers (which is also qatar's national dialing number), will be completely dismantled and shipped to a still-yet-to-be-decided-upon country in need of a second-hand sports stadium.
